Layer chickens

Layer chickens are a particular species of hens which need to be raised from one day old, these chickens are also known for their egg-laying capacity. Popular laying breeds include the White Leghorn, Red Sex Link and Black Sex Link breeds. A healthy hen will lay eggs for several years. Hens begin to lay at approximately 16-20 weeks of age. They remain to lay eggs continuously till 72-78 weeks of age. They can produce about one kg of eggs by consuming about 2.25 kg of food during their egg-laying period. To produce hybrid eggs layer, consider the various characteristics of cock and hen before breeding.

Chicken Type Average Eggs/year
Bovans brown 250-300
Leghorns 280-320
Orpingtons 200-280

Meet chickens(Broiler)

A broiler is any chicken that is breed and raised specifically for meat production(Meat-producing chickens are very efficient at converting feed to meat). Most commercial broilers reach slaughter weight between four and six weeks of age, although slower growing breeds reach slaughter weight at approximately 14 weeks of age. They producing approximately one the pound of bodyweight for every two pounds of feed they eat. Meat-producing chickens are broad breasted and larger than the laying breeds.

Broiler Type Mature Weight/avg(lb)
Cornish Cross 9-12
Big Red Broilers 7-10
Kosher King 5-7

Chicken Eggs

Chicken egg is a main component of the human diet serving as a dietary source of protein, fat, and other nutrients. The chicken egg is made up of approximately two-thirds white and one-third yolk. The yolk contains lipids, vitamins, minerals, and carotenoid pigments. It is considered a rich source of bioavailable lutein and zeaxanthin carotenoids which are present only in the egg yolk.

Chicken Egg Average Weight/g
Leghorn's egg 55
Bovans brown's egg 63.2
Orpington's egg 58
